Changhua–Kaohsiung Viaduct

History

The Changhua–Kaohsiung Viaduct is located in Taiwan and serves the Taiwan High-Speed Rail network. It is approximately 157 kilometers long and is one of the longest bridges in the world. Engineers designed the bridge to withstand earthquakes, making it one of the safest railway structures. The viaduct plays a vital role in connecting major cities and improving transportation efficiency across Taiwan.
